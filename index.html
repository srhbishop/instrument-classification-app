Classify the Instruments

An exercise in Hornbostel-Sachs Instrument Classification

In the early 20th century, musicologists Hornbostel and Sachs developed a means of scientifically classifying musical instruments according to the manner in which those instruments vibrate. They came up with four broad categories:

  1. Idiophones - instruments that produce sound when the entire body of the instrument vibrates (e.g. woodblocks)
  2. Membranophones - instruments that produce sound when a stretched membrane vibrates (e.g. most drums)
  3. Chordophones - instruments that produce sound when a string vibrates. May be struck (e.g. guitar) or bowed (e.g. violin)
  4. Aerophones - instruments that produce sound when vibration is caused by air passing through a tube (e.g. flute)

More recently, a 5th class has been added called electrophone to account for instruments that produce sound from an electrical impulse (e.g. electric keyboards and guitars, drum machines).
